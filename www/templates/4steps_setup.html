The Four Steps

When you get in a place of pain or stress, use the following 4 Steps to build new pathways in your brain that lead to different reactions. It’s important to say the steps out loud to your spouse. Verbalizing your 4 Steps moves you from the emotional part of your brain (amygdala) to the thinking part of your brain (prefrontal cortex), where you have the ability to choose a different response.

HELPFUL HINT:

Your Pain Cycle will remind you of your typical feelings and ways of reacting when you’re in pain (Steps 1 and 2). Your Peace Cycle will remind you of your typical truths and new actions (Steps 3 and 4).

  • 1
    SAY WHAT YOU FEEL
    (Important: don’t add to this statement; adding a
    “because you…” will only start another argument)
  • 2SAY WHAT YOU NORMALLY DO
  • 3SAY THE TRUTH
  • 4SAY WHAT YOU WILL DO DIFFERENTLY
